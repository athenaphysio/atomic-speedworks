About the programme

Speed is made, not born.

A clear, structured programme that helps your child get measurably faster, stronger and more confident over six weeks of the school summer holidays — built by a chartered physiotherapist with a PhD in youth rugby and coached alongside a former professional and Olympic athlete.

Speed is the single most impactful quality in sport. It decides whether a player makes the tackle, beats the defender, or is the one who recovers first. It is also the quality most often left to chance in youth sport. Young players are often told to "run more" — but not actually coached on how to develop and harness speed.

ASW is six weeks of structured, age-appropriate strength and speed coaching. Over the 12 sessions your child will:

Get Faster Over 10m

Become measurably quicker over 10 metres — the most sport-relevant kind of speed.

Harness Sprint Mechanics

Improve posture, knee drive, stride frequency and ground contact.

Build Strength & Power

Build lower-limb strength and jumping power that protect against injury.

Master Change of Direction

Sharpen change-of-direction and deceleration ability.

Understand the Why

Athletes who understand are athletes who improve.

Week by week

How the programme works

Two sessions a week for six weeks — twelve sessions in total. Each session is between 35 and 50 minutes including warm-up and cool-down.

What "video-guided" actually means

On each video-guided session, your child opens the Atomic app and presses play. They are walked through an activation, the main exercises and a cool-down by a coach on screen. Every exercise has a short demonstration video. They tick the session off when they are done. They can leave a comment for their coaches through the app.

The two testing days

What actually happens

Testing is what makes ASW different. You and your child want improvement, and we hand you the numbers that show it.

Testing dates — Silver, Gold & VIP

If you cannot make these dates we will do all we can to add additional testing sessions within those two weeks. Please email David@DrDavidSilverPhD.com to request.

Day 1 — Baseline testing (at Cobham Rugby)

Your child arrives in normal training kit. The session lasts around 40 minutes and parents are welcome on the sideline. We measure five things. Day 1 finishes with a short briefing for parents — how the next sessions work, a walk-through of the app, and a Q&A.

Day 12 — Retest (at the club)

We repeat the same five tests in the same order. We compare your child's numbers against their own baseline. Each athlete and parent receives a report with the numbers, the percentage change, and a short coach commentary. The session closes with a friendly relay and a group photo.

What we measureWhat that means in plain English
10-metre sprint, timed by laser gatesHow quickly they accelerate — the first phase of every sprint in rugby.
30-metre sprintWhether they are limited by acceleration or by top speed.
Countermovement jump (CMJ)How much power their legs produce, measured in cm of jump height. Each leg and both together.
Peak Force Isometric PullHow much force they can produce in extension.
5-0-5 change-of-direction testHow quickly they can stop, turn and re-accelerate. The cut every sports player has to make.
Safety

How we look after your child

Every parent should ask whether a programme is safe. The honest answer is, nothing in sport is injury risk-free, but ASW is built specifically to reduce injury risks.

  • Every exercise is written and screened by a chartered physiotherapist (David). Nothing in the programme is more demanding than a well-coached training session at any junior sports club.
  • Every session has a structured warm-up and cool-down. Your child is never asked to sprint cold.
  • Volume is age-appropriate. Foundation (12–14): fewer reps, lower load, more mechanics. Performance (15–18): higher intensity.
  • A "pain-stop" rule is built into every video. If something hurts beyond normal training fatigue, your child stops the session and messages the coach. We respond within 24 hours.
  • You will be asked to complete an important medical info check for your child prior to enrolment.
What your child needs from you

Designed for a busy summer

The less your child relies on you, the better the programme works. Here is what you actually need to provide:

  • Time: about 45 minutes twice a week, plus the two in-person testing days.
  • Resistance bands (light, medium, heavy) — around £15–£25.
  • One pair of dumbbells (5–12.5 kg) or a single kettlebell — around £20–£40.
  • 3m × 2m of clear level floor space.
  • A chair or box (or stairs).
  • A phone or tablet to follow the videos.
  • A garden, park, pitch or green space.
  • Foundation athletes (12–14) can complete almost the entire programme without dumbbells if you'd rather avoid the spend.

Encouragement, not enforcement. The athletes who get the most out of this are the ones whose parents check in on the weekly progress and ask their child what they liked — not the ones standing over them with a stopwatch. The coaching is our job. Your job is to cheerlead.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

My child has never done strength work. Is this too advanced?
No. The Foundation stream (ages 12–14) is bodyweight and bands only. We teach movement before we add load. Many of our athletes have never picked up a dumbbell before the programme.
My child is already in an academy or county pathway. Will they be challenged?
Yes. The Performance stream (ages 15–18) introduces sprints, loaded single-leg work, and reactive strength training that is consistent with professional academies. The Gold and VIP tiers add personalised mid-programme reviews.
What's the difference between Gold and VIP?
Gold gives your child one 30-minute review call with David or Marcus. VIP gives your child two 30-minute calls — one with David focused on strength and movement, and one with Marcus focused on sprint mechanics and the running side. VIP doubles the personalised coaching time and gives your child face-time with both coaches.
What if my child is injured during the programme?
Injuries that occur during a programme session are reviewed immediately by David. Most niggles can be managed with a one-week modification. Significant injuries are referred into appropriate care — the same care David provides every day in his clinic.
My child has summer sports commitments. Is this too much?
We design ASW to fit around summer holidays, club tours, and family time. The home sessions are short and flexible. If your child has a heavy week (a tour, for example), they can drop a session and the app rolls everything forward.
What if my child cannot make the in-person testing day?
If the conflict is known in advance, we can usually offer a make-up testing slot in the same week. If not, the Bronze tier is appropriate and includes the full video-guided programme.
Do you offer a refund?
If your child cannot start the cohort for any reason, we offer a full refund up to 14 days before the cohort start. After that, we offer a transfer to next summer's cohort.
How are video sessions delivered?
Through the Atomic app on a phone, tablet or laptop. Each session is a single short video your child follows along with — no extra browsing, no in-app purchases, no advertising.
Is my and my child's data private?
Yes. The Atomic app does not record any medical data. Test results are shared with you, the parent, and stored securely. They are never sold or shared with third parties.
Can my child do ASW without playing a pitch-based sport?
We welcome athletes from any background or ability. This is about getting better at anything that moves. Please email us first with any questions.
When does the next ASW summer cohort run?
ASW runs once a year only, across the summer holidays into early autumn. Day 1 is in early July, Day 12 is in early September. Bookings open in May. If you miss this summer, the next cohort is twelve months later.
What happens after the six weeks?
Your child can stop there with their progress report and what they have learned. Or they can continue with ASW Year-Round to keep building through the season. The choice is yours, made at the end of Day 12.
